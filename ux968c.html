Persona: Pat Planner

To help understand the context of the software, here is one of our primary personas: Pat Planner.

Pat Planner

"This convention is going to be the most inclusive and diverse one of its kind yet!"

Tools and Technology

  • Expects to do all of planning on Desktop as opposed to Mobile
  • Online form/survey tool (Google Forms, Survey Monkey)
  • Word Processor (Microsoft Word, Google Docs)
  • Spreadsheet Program (Microsoft Excel, Google Sheets)
  • Email (Gmail, Office365 Outlook)
  • Calendar software (Google Calendar)

Demographics

  • Adult
  • Attends and volunteers for fan conventions
  • Reads and writes English fluently
  • Note: there is no particular age group, background, or gender associated with this persona.

Attributes

  • Conscious of social justice
  • Volunteering for conventions is a huge part of their life

Pat works a boring 9 to 5 job, and then comes home and spends the rest of the evening volunteering for conventions. Their friends and family are also involved, so "unrelated" gatherings often turn into impromptu meetings. Pat needs software that will help plan the convention program, keeping all the relevant information secure and in one place.

Pat has a few concerns about any software they are going to use for the convention. First and foremost, the privacy of participants is very important, especially as there are many famous authors. Also, many times in the past participants have found themselves assigned to program items that make them feel uncomfortable, or that they had little or no interest in, so following a consent-based model for assigning participants to program items is key.

Having a more diverse and inclusive convention is very important to Pat. Diverse and inclusive language is important to avoid alienating people, and diversity metrics are important to be able to track progress, or lack thereof. Any software needs to include both of those. All content should have a robust diversity review process, to help mitigate the impact of unconscious bias.

Task background: Survey Creation

One of the tasks at the beginning of a planning cycle is to contact potential program participants and ask them if they're interested in being part of the convention program. Ideally, information about the demographics, interests, and online persona (social media or web presence) would also be collected, to assist in the vetting and diversity review process. Usually, this information is collected using an online survey.

The survey generally consists of several types of questions: short and long answer, multiple choice, and some fields that need specifically formatted information, such as email or physical address. It also needs some basic branching capability, so it can display different sets of questions based on a respondent's previous answers.

Ideally, responses from a user would be tied to their person record in the system. That would allow the planner to view the information in a unified place. Also, the respondent could log back into the system and edit their answers if their information changes.

I write the questions beforehand in a shared word processor document. It's easier to make comments asychronously, and no one is really focused on the convention just yet. Everyone is already familiar with how to access shared documents. I don't really expect the software to re-implement Google Docs.
Wow! This looks like real software. Create works just like I expect it to. It's very snappy!
I don't quite understand what some of these buttons are supposed to do, or how I can accomplish common tasks. Some of them are fine though.
There is no WYSIWYG formatting capability at all, so I was told to hand-write the html if I want formatting to appear in my questions. I don't know html!
I don't understand how pages or branching are supposed to work. They don't make any sense to me. I think something might be broken?
If I'm editing the page, and someone else edits the page, it's a race to see whose changes stick. This means I have to check with others outside the application.
I can preview my changes in a separate tab. Every time I make a change, I can refresh, and see what it looks like now.
I'm adding users in order to invite them to pilot the survey. It is about as easy as I expect.
In order to get someone a click-to-login survey link, I have to ask a developer. As a workaround, I can just send the piloter the survey link, and instructions on how to reset their password, which they have to do before they can take the survey. However, that is messy.
Publishing the survey is easy, and has the expected warning dialogs. This is what I expect from survey software.

Insights

In general, we could help Pat a lot by including a WYSIWYG editor and an easier way to link to the survey for piloting purposes. There are also clearly still some bugs that need to be worked out. (This journey was constructed from several conversations I had with our first users during their initial encounters with the software.)
